Publix's $83 Million Polo Club Shops Acquisition: What It Means for Boca Raton

A Deep Dive into This Major Commercial Real Estate Transaction

Samantha Gornstein

Real Estate Professional | Market Analyst
January 2026

Publix Super Markets recently completed the purchase of Polo Club Shops—the retail center on Military Trail that the grocer has anchored for years. The purchase price was approximately $83 million, according to The Real Deal and other commercial real estate sources.

This acquisition is part of a broader Publix strategy: buying shopping centers it already anchors to gain more control over property management, tenant mix, and rental income. It's a move we're seeing the company make across Florida, and it signals long-term confidence in specific locations.

The Numbers Behind the Deal

Polo Club Shops, located at 5560 Military Trail in Boca Raton, spans approximately 140,000 square feet and sits on roughly 17 acres. The $83 million sale price represents about $593 per square foot—a strong valuation for an anchored retail property in South Florida.

For context, the seller—Jamestown Properties—originally purchased the property in 2015 for approximately $50.6 million. That means Jamestown realized a gain of over 64% on the investment in roughly a decade, not accounting for rental income during the holding period.

Transaction Highlights

  • Purchase Price: $83 million
  • Price Per Square Foot: ~$593
  • Property Size: ~140,000 square feet
  • Land Area: ~17 acres
  • Seller's Return: 64% gain since 2015

Why This Matters for Polo Club and Boca Raton

Publix's Strategy: Tenant to Owner

The property is nearly fully leased and performs strongly due to its Boca Raton location and diverse tenant mix. With Publix holding a long-term lease (20 years, according to Shopping Center Business), the center has been an attractive, stable asset for investors—and now, Publix itself has decided it's worth owning outright.

For shoppers and existing tenants, this ownership change likely means continued strong stewardship. Publix has a reputation for maintaining high standards at centers it controls, and there's potential for additional investment in amenities and property improvements over time.

Location Advantage

Polo Club Shops benefits from its proximity to The Polo Club—one of South Florida's premier country club communities. The center serves not only Polo Club residents but also draws from surrounding neighborhoods in West Boca and Delray Beach.

The Clint Moore Road and Military Trail intersection continues to see strong retail performance, and this acquisition by Publix reinforces the area's long-term viability as a commercial corridor.

Implications for Commercial Real Estate Investors

This high-value sale reflects ongoing investor confidence in Boca Raton's retail market. When institutional buyers—especially one as deliberate as Publix—commit $83 million to a single property, it reinforces that this area is viewed as a stable, appreciating asset.

For commercial property owners and investors in the area, Publix's decision to buy (rather than simply renew its lease) serves as a strong signal: retail centers in well-located, affluent South Florida markets remain attractive long-term investments.

The Bottom Line: Publix's $83 million investment is a vote of confidence in Boca Raton. For those of us who live, work, and sell real estate here, it's a welcome sign of continued strength in our market.

What This Means for Polo Club Residential Real Estate

While this is a commercial transaction, it has residential implications. Strong retail infrastructure supports residential property values. When a major grocer like Publix invests heavily in maintaining and potentially improving a nearby shopping center, it enhances the overall appeal of living in the area.

For Polo Club homeowners and prospective buyers, this transaction is one more data point suggesting that the area's fundamentals—location, amenities, and commercial stability—remain strong.
