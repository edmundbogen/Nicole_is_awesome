Publix Buys Polo Club Shops for $83M

A Major Ownership Change and What It Signals for Boca Raton Retail

By Samantha Gornstein | The Edmund Bogen Team at Douglas Elliman Real Estate

When a grocery chain pays $83 million to own the shopping center it already anchors, that's not just a real estate transactionit's a strategic statement about the future of a neighborhood.

What Happened?

Publix Super Markets recently completed the purchase of Polo Club Shopsthe retail center on Military Trail that the grocer has anchored for years. The purchase price was approximately $83 million, according to The Real Deal and other commercial real estate sources.

This acquisition is part of a broader Publix strategy: buying shopping centers it already anchors to gain more control over property management, tenant mix, and rental income. It's a move we're seeing the company make across Florida, and it signals long-term confidence in specific locations.

Key Fact: The purchase price was substantially higher than what the previous owner paid about a decade ago, reflecting strong investor interest in well-located retail centers in South Florida.

Why This Matters for the Community

The property is nearly fully leased and performs strongly due to its Boca Raton location and diverse tenant mix. With Publix holding a long-term lease (20 years, according to Shopping Center Business), the center has been an attractive, stable asset for investorsand now, Publix itself has decided it's worth owning outright.

For shoppers and existing tenants, this ownership change likely means continued strong stewardship. Publix has a reputation for maintaining high standards at centers it controls, and there's potential for additional investment in amenities and property improvements over time.

New Dining Option on the Way

In addition to the ownership news, Beignets & Brew is opening a location at Polo Club Shops. The concept will serve traditional and specialty beignets alongside coffee and other beverages. While an official opening date hasn't been announced, local planning and permitting are already underway, according to Tap Into.

This adds to the dining variety at the center, complementing existing favorites like First Watch.

What Could This Mean for Boca Raton Real Estate?

Here's where it gets interestingand where my perspective as a realtor who sells in this area every day matters most.

This high-value sale reflects ongoing investor confidence in Boca Raton's retail market. When institutional buyersespecially one as deliberate as Publixcommit $83 million to a single property, it reinforces that this area is viewed as a stable, appreciating asset.

For homeowners near Polo Club Shops, this is a positive signal. Well-maintained, thriving retail centers support property values in surrounding neighborhoods. Buyers evaluating homes in the area can take comfort in knowing that a major anchor is investedliterallyin the long-term success of the location.

For sellers, this is another data point that demonstrates Boca Raton's enduring appeal. Strong commercial investment often correlates with strong residential demand.

The Bottom Line: Publix's $83 million investment is a vote of confidence in Boca Raton. For those of us who live, work, and sell real estate here, it's a welcome sign of continued strength in our market.
